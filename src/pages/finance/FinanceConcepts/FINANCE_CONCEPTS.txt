────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. LEDGER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DEFINITION
  A Ledger is the master record of all financial transactions for a single
  account (a vendor, customer, employee, or expense head). Every transaction
  that touches that account gets posted as a line entry — debit or credit —
  and the running balance tells you exactly how much is owed/receivable at
  any point in time.

HOW A LEDGER LOOKS
  Vendor: ABC Suppliers Pvt Ltd  (VND-002)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Date        Particulars               Debit (Dr)   Credit (Cr)   Balance
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  2026-03-01  Opening Balance                                        ₹0
  2026-03-10  Purchase Bill PB/25-26/0001              ₹23,990      ₹23,990 Cr
  2026-03-15  Credit Note CN/25-26/001    ₹1,416                    ₹22,574 Cr
  2026-03-18  Debit Note DN/25-26/001     ₹2,500                    ₹20,074 Cr
  2026-03-20  Payment Voucher PV/001      ₹20,074                   ₹0
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Cr balance = you OWE the vendor (liability)
  Dr balance = vendor owes you (overpayment or excess credit note)

TYPES OF LEDGERS IN CONSTRUCTION ERP
  ┌─────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Ledger Type         │ Examples                               │
  ├─────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Vendor / Creditor   │ ABC Suppliers, Steel Corp              │
  │ Client / Debtor     │ NHAI, PWD, Municipal Corp              │
  │ Expense Head        │ Material Cost, Labour, Transport       │
  │ Bank / Cash         │ HDFC Current A/c, Petty Cash           │
  │ Tax                 │ CGST Payable, SGST Payable, TDS        │
  └─────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘

DOUBLE-ENTRY ACCOUNTING (every transaction creates two entries)

  Purchase Bill raised:
    Dr  Material/Expense A/c      ₹20,000
    Dr  CGST Input A/c             ₹1,800
    Dr  SGST Input A/c             ₹1,800
    Cr  Vendor Ledger (ABC)       ₹23,600   ← liability created

  Payment made:
    Dr  Vendor Ledger (ABC)       ₹23,600   ← liability cleared
    Cr  Bank A/c                  ₹23,600

HOW THE LEDGER IS COMPUTED (for our ERP)
  Rather than a stored document, the vendor ledger is a LIVE AGGREGATE
  computed on-the-fly from:
    + Purchase Bills (Cr entries)
    - Credit Notes   (Dr entries — reduce payable)
    - Debit Notes    (Dr entries — reduce payable)
    - Payment Vouchers (Dr entries — clear payable)

  Net Payable = Σ PB amounts − Σ CN amounts − Σ DN amounts − Σ Payments


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. CREDIT NOTE (CN)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DEFINITION
  A Credit Note is a document issued by the VENDOR TO YOU (or by you to a
  client) to REDUCE an outstanding liability/receivable. It is a partial or
  full reversal of an original invoice/purchase bill.

WHEN A VENDOR SENDS YOU A CREDIT NOTE
  - You returned damaged or excess material (material return)
  - The vendor overcharged on an invoice (price correction)
  - Quantity short-supplied vs what was billed
  - Post-invoice discount granted by the vendor

EFFECT ON VENDOR LEDGER
  Before CN:  Vendor balance = ₹23,990 Cr  (you owe ₹23,990)
  CN raised:  ₹1,416 Dr entry posted to vendor ledger
  After CN:   Vendor balance = ₹22,574 Cr  (you now owe ₹22,574)

  → CN reduces your payable. It is a partial reversal of the original bill.

CREDIT NOTE DOCUMENT STRUCTURE
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ CN No:        CN/25-26/001                                     │
  │ Date:         2026-03-15                                       │
  │ Against Bill: PB/25-26/0001  (original purchase bill)         │
  │ Vendor:       ABC Suppliers Pvt Ltd  (VND-002)                 │
  │ Tender:       TND-001                                          │
  │                                                                │
  │ Line Items:                                                    │
  │   Item           Qty  Rate  Gross   CGST   SGST   Net         │
  │   Cement OPC 53   3   400   1,200    108    108   1,416       │
  │                                                                │
  │ Reason: 3 bags returned — damaged on delivery                  │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

GST ON CREDIT NOTES
  - GST must be reversed on the CN amount
  - You LOSE the input tax credit (ITC) you had originally claimed
  - CGST / SGST / IGST are recalculated on the CN line items
  - Vendor must issue a GST-compliant CN with their GSTIN
  - Required for ITC reversal reflected in GSTR-2B

KEY FIELDS FOR SCHEMA
  - cn_no          (auto-generated: CN/<FY>/<seq>)
  - cn_date
  - bill_ref       (ObjectId → PurchaseBill)
  - bill_no        (snapshot of PB doc_id)
  - vendor_id / vendor_ref / vendor_name / vendor_gstin (snapshots)
  - tender_id / tender_ref / tender_name
  - line_items[]   (item_id, item_description, unit, return_qty,
                    unit_price, gross_amt, cgst_pct, sgst_pct, igst_pct,
                    cgst_amt, sgst_amt, igst_amt, net_amt)
  - reason         (free text: "Damaged", "Excess", "Price Diff", etc.)
  - grand_total / total_tax / net_amount  (computed by pre-save)
  - status         (draft → pending → approved)


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. DEBIT NOTE (DN)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DEFINITION
  A Debit Note is the mirror image of a Credit Note. It is used to record
  a REDUCTION in payable due to a claim you raise against the vendor
  (penalty, short supply, price difference), OR an INCREASE in payable when
  the vendor raises a DN against you (supplementary charges).

TWO SCENARIOS

  Scenario A — YOU raise a DN against the vendor
    Used when you want to formally claim a deduction:
    - Penalty for delayed supply
    - Quality failure / rejection at site
    - Price difference (vendor billed higher than agreed PO rate)
    - Short supply deduction
    Effect: Dr entry in your books against the vendor
            → reduces what you pay them

  Scenario B — VENDOR raises a DN against you
    Used when the vendor needs to charge you extra beyond original bill:
    - Additional freight / handling not in original bill
    - Price escalation approved post-PO
    - Supplementary supply quantity
    Effect: Cr entry — increases your payable to vendor

EFFECT ON VENDOR LEDGER (Scenario A — you raise DN for penalty)
  Before DN:  Vendor balance = ₹22,574 Cr
  DN raised:  ₹2,500 Dr entry posted
  After DN:   Vendor balance = ₹20,074 Cr

DEBIT NOTE DOCUMENT STRUCTURE
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ DN No:        DN/25-26/001                                     │
  │ Date:         2026-03-18                                       │
  │ Against Bill: PB/25-26/0001                                    │
  │ Vendor:       ABC Suppliers Pvt Ltd  (VND-002)                 │
  │ Tender:       TND-001                                          │
  │                                                                │
  │ Particulars:                                                   │
  │   Penalty for 5-day delay in supply @ ₹500/day = ₹2,500       │
  │                                                                │
  │ Net Deduction: ₹2,500                                          │
  │ Raised by: Romaa (company)                                     │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

GST ON DEBIT NOTES
  - If you raise DN for penalty/price diff: typically NO GST (penalty is
    not a supply of goods/services)
  - If vendor raises DN for supplementary supply: GST applicable,
    treated like a supplementary bill — adds to your ITC

KEY FIELDS FOR SCHEMA
  - dn_no          (auto-generated: DN/<FY>/<seq>)
  - dn_date
  - bill_ref       (ObjectId → PurchaseBill)
  - bill_no        (snapshot)
  - raised_by      ("company" | "vendor")
  - vendor_id / vendor_ref / vendor_name / vendor_gstin
  - tender_id / tender_ref / tender_name
  - particulars[]  (description, amount, gst_pct, net)
  - reason_type    (enum: "Penalty", "PriceDiff", "ShortSupply",
                    "Freight", "Escalation", "Other")
  - net_amount     (computed)
  - status         (draft → pending → approved)

4. HOW THEY INTER-RELATE IN CONSTRUCTION ERP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  GRN (material received)
      ↓
  Purchase Bill (PB) ──────────────────── Vendor Ledger: +₹23,990 Cr
      │
      ├── Credit Note (CN) ─────────────── Vendor Ledger: -₹1,416 Cr
      │   (material return / overbilling)
      │
      ├── Debit Note (DN) ──────────────── Vendor Ledger: -₹2,500 Cr
      │   (penalty / price difference)
      │
      └── Payment Voucher (PV) ─────────── Vendor Ledger: -₹20,074 Cr
          (net payment)                    Bank Ledger:   -₹20,074 Dr

  Net Payable = ₹23,990 (PB) − ₹1,416 (CN) − ₹2,500 (DN) = ₹20,074

  │                    │                          │ Increases it (vendor)    │
  ├────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Common trigger     │ Material return,         │ Penalty, price diff,     │
  │                    │ overbilling, discount    │ short supply, escalation │
  ├────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ GST impact         │ ITC reversed             │ Usually no GST (penalty) │
  │                    │                          │ New ITC (vendor DN)      │
  ├────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Always links to    │ Original Purchase Bill   │ Original Purchase Bill   │
  └────────────────────┴─────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────┘

6. SCOPING DECISIONS BEFORE SCHEMA DESIGN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The following questions must be aligned on before writing schemas:

  Q1. BILL-LINKED OR STANDALONE?
      → Must every CN/DN always reference a PurchaseBill?
      → Or can CNs/DNs exist standalone (e.g. advance deductions, opening bal)?

  Q2. DIRECTION — WHO RAISES?
      → Do we track only CNs/DNs received from vendors?
      → Or also CNs/DNs raised by Romaa against vendors?
      → Or CNs/DNs raised by Romaa against clients (separate flow)?

  Q3. LEDGER STORAGE — LIVE vs STORED?
      → Option A: Compute ledger on-the-fly by aggregating PB + CN + DN + PV
        (simpler to maintain, always accurate, slower query)
      → Option B: Store a LedgerEntry collection with posted entries
        (faster reads, needs careful write discipline, risk of drift)
      Recommendation: Option A (aggregate) for now — add materialised
      LedgerEntry later when reporting volume demands it.

  Q4. PAYMENT VOUCHER (PV) — IN SCOPE NOW?
      → PV settles the net vendor balance (Dr vendor, Cr bank)
      → Without PV, the ledger balance never reaches zero
      → Minimum viable: track PV as a simple record with amount + bill refs

  Q5. GST REVERSAL TRACKING?
      → For GST filing (GSTR-2), ITC reversed on CNs must be trackable
      → Needs: cn_line_items to carry cgst_amt, sgst_amt, igst_amt
      → Already planned in CN schema above

  Q6. PARTIAL CN/DN?
      → Can a CN cover only some items from the original bill?
      → Can a DN cover only a partial amount?
      → Recommendation: Yes — line-item level CN, amount-level DN

  Q7. APPROVAL WORKFLOW?
      → Status: draft → pending → approved
      → Who approves? (role-based — finance manager)
      → Does an approved CN/DN automatically post to ledger?
